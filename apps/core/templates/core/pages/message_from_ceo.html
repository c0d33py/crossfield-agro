Direct from the CEO

"A farmer doesn't buy a leveler or a pivot for the brochure. They buy it because the season starts on a fixed date and the equipment has to be ready. Everything we do has to serve that."

Crosfield Agro has been supplying Pakistani farms since 1990 — and growers further afield for nearly as long. The work is unglamorous and unforgiving: a centre pivot that won't start at 4 a.m., a tractor that throws a part during harvest, a leveler that arrived at the wrong site. Our job is to make sure those things don't happen, and to be there when they do.

We've kept the company close to the field on purpose. Branches across Pakistan rather than one big showroom. Engineers who have spent time on tractors and pivots themselves. Components manufactured locally where it improves price, lead time or spare-parts availability — and partnerships with international brands where it doesn't.

We're investing in three things over the next few years. Water — because every irrigation system we sell is one less farm pumping more than it needs to. Power — because solar is finally cheap enough to displace diesel for most pumping loads. And service — because the gap between selling a machine and supporting it for ten years is where most equipment companies fail their customers.

If you're evaluating Crosfield as a supplier, the best thing you can do is visit a branch, look at the parts shelves, and talk to the engineers. We've built the business so that scrutiny is welcome.

— Chief Executive Officer, Crosfield Agro
